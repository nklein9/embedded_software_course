Detailed Description

The main file for the Noodle Arm Trap.

Created on: April 27, 2019

Author
Nicholas Klein

The NoodleArm Trap is one of several traps desgined for the Thief Simulator final project by the Embedded Software class of Spring 2019. The goal of the NoodleArm Trap is to impede the progress of someone trying to pass through the arc of a spinning pool noodle by hitting them with it. It is comprised of A Pool Noodle on an arm which is rotated horizontally by a brushed motor raised on a stand. The motor direction is controlled by an H-bridge which recieves its signal from an MSP430F5526 Microcontroller. When the arm makes an impact, a vibration sensor on the stand detects the impact causing the microcontroller to switch the direction of current through the motor, changing the direction of rotation. Each impact with the noodle sends a time penalty to the Brainframe of the Thief Simulator equal to the programmed difficulty in seconds. Difficulty is 10 by default but can be altered by the Brainframe.
